## One-Paragraph Description

Retail MerchBench is a retail AI benchmark and model-routing toolkit for merchandise-planning workflows. It evaluates whether a model is economically sufficient for a task once quality, latency, inference cost, downside risk, reversibility, deterministic controls, and human review are considered. The repo includes a 100-item retail eval corpus, 14-model scored panel, reproducible runner, visual Atlas, and full research paper.

## LinkedIn Post

Retail AI teams should not route every workflow to the strongest model by default.

They need to decide when to use rules, small models, mid-tier models, frontier models, cascades, or human review.

I published Retail MerchBench to make that decision measurable. It includes:

- 100 retail-native eval items across eight planning workflow segments
- 14 scored model runs across local, hosted, and paid providers
- deterministic adversarial baselines
- a reproducible model runner
- a visual Atlas for cost, quality, and workflow routing
- a full research paper

The core idea: the right model is the cheapest safe workflow that clears the quality floor and escalates the risky cases.

GitHub: https://github.com/Novice-ninja/retail-merchbench
